Why Olhão ?

The Shellfish Capital

The municipal markets of Olhão, one of the ex-libris of the town of Olhão, began to be built in 1912 and were inaugurated four years later. For nearly a century, they have been one of the illustrated postcards of Olhão and a must for tourists and residents.

The historic quarter is one of the most important places to be seen and visited in Olhão because of its typicity and history. The cubist architecture is unique in the country.

My favourite cafés

Figo de Pita

Serves meat, vegan options available. Health food restaurant offering vegan raw spring rolls, fried sweet potatoes, burgers, smoothies and a variety of tofu bowls. Tue-Sat 09:30-17:00, Sun 09:30-15:00.

Address

R.Moinho da Baretta 48, 8700-431 Olhão

What I like about it

It is a really cosy place with a good vibe. The meals are very colourful and more importantly, delicious! It is great for meat eaters but also for vegan eaters. I just love it.

Esquina da Baixa

Here, there’s a spot for everyone. The menu boasts a diverse selection, ranging from delectable bruschettas and tacos to mouthwatering pancakes and nourishing bowls. Don’t miss out on their refreshing variety of natural juices, perfect for quenching your thirst on a sunny day.

Address

Av.5 de Outubro 78, 8700-431 Olhão

What I like about it

This is a great place to go if you like to brunch and also enjoy the lively market activity.

Delícia de Olhão

Here, you can experience a truly local breakfast, frequented mostly by the locals themselves, with a more traditional vibe. There is incredible pastries and a wide selection of options that’ll make your breakfast experience feel like a feast. The ambiance has a cozy, cafeteria-style feel, adding to the authentic charm of this beloved spot.

Address

Av.5 de Outubro 20A, 8700-431 Olhão

What I like about it

It is a great place to feel the local vibe.
